## What Makes a Great Roofing Web Design?

### Search Engine Optimization

To succeed at [roofing SEO](https://contractorgorilla.com/roofing/seo/), your site needs a well-organized structure with meaningful internal links, and it must function well and look great on any screen size, including mobile devices. Ideally, the time it takes for a page to load shouldn’t exceed two seconds. The steps to stay under this threshold include reformatting images, using a cache plug-in to enhance loading speed, and hosting videos on a third-party platform, such as YouTube.

Substantive content is also critical for high search rankings. Landing pages, blog posts, and the site’s meta content need to incorporate well-researched and carefully selected keywords relevant to roofing. High-quality photos, videos, graphics, and other visuals improve SEO as well. Also, it’s important to emphasize geographic location to boost local SEO.

### User Friendliness

SEO principles contribute to user friendliness. For example, pages that load more quickly have a lower bounce rate, because website visitors are less likely to get frustrated by the slow performance and hit the back button. A user-friendly site is also functional on all devices, such as smartphones. Customers should never encounter text that’s unreadable and buttons or links that are too small or spaced too close together to click on easily.

Site visitors should also be able to navigate the site easily and find the specific roofing service they need or the topic they’re interested in further exploring. The interactive features, such as forms, must always work properly, and there can’t be any broken links.

### Customer Conversion

The ultimate test of your site’s success is whether it’s generating leads and sales. A strong website is persuasive and memorable, using attractive, compelling visuals and text to draw people in, inspire trust, and deliver value. It also features prominent call-to-action content that prompts customers to fulfill desirable goals. A CTA button may tell them to call you, and a contact form may urge them to enter their email for a free roof inspection or estimate.

Analytics tools give you insights into how customers are arriving at your site, moving through it, interacting with its content, and reaching the goals that you’ve set. To maintain a strong website, you need to keep tweaking or optimizing it to strengthen conversion rates.

## How to Choose a Roofing Website Designer

### Highly Developed Skills and a Strong Track Record

Start by evaluating the skills and experience of the company you’re looking into. Go over their previous work, which may be available as a portfolio, and read reviews and testimonials about them. Check if they produce websites that look homogeneous or if they can convey your brand personality and uniqueness.

### Specialization

Pick a company that specializes in contractor websites and has successfully worked on sites for roofing businesses. Specialization means that the designer understands the requirements of roofing companies and what potential customers look for when they land on your site. For example, a skillful designer should know the best ways to design and position CTA content.

### Marketing Expertise

Another critical quality to look for in a web designer is an understanding of marketing. Your website is more than an attractive representation of your brand. It’s a core part of your content marketing. A web designer needs to possess up-to-date knowledge on how to optimize your site for SEO and customer conversion, and they should also be familiar with the best metrics for determining whether the site is performing optimally.

### Good Values

Hire a company that operates with transparency and integrity. Your web designer should be able to account for the costs of your site and justify each decision, explaining how it benefits your site’s performance and your marketing goals. Furthermore, if they say they’ll commit to a certain schedule and stay within a budget you’ve both agreed on, they need to follow through on those promises.

## How Much Do Roofing Websites Cost?

Hiring a professional to build your website from scratch will typically cost somewhere between $2,500 and $10,000. However, the exact costs depend on a range of variables.

**Size.** Maybe you envision an attractive site with a basic layout and relatively few pages. Generally, this setup will cost less than a site with many pages and a more complex structure.

**Web hosting.** Your choice of hosting plan affects how much storage you have, the speed of your site, and whether or not your site will be prone to protracted downtime. Various web hosts offer cheap prices for plans, but you need to check that a cheaper plan won’t undermine your site’s performance and damage its SEO.

**Security.** What kinds of security features does your site need? At minimum, you need a Secure Sockets Layer certificate. The average annual cost of an SSL certificate is $60, but your site may require other security features. For example, you may want to integrate a payment portal with your site.

**Content.** You may need to hire a professional writer for landing pages and blog posts. For visual content, you may need a professional photographer, videographer, and graphic designer. Even if you perform at least some of the work on your own, you may have to pay for software and special equipment, such as cameras and microphones.

**Complexity.** The features you display, the layout you choose, the software you integrate with the site, and the complexity of the code also affect the price, including how much it will cost to optimize your site for SEO and update it when necessary.

To get a clear picture of how much your site will cost, it’s best to consult with a professional. Because so many details depend on your particular needs and preferences, you can get an accurate estimate only when you review the specifics with a web designer.

### How much does it cost to design a roofing website?

Hiring a professional to design and build your website will typically cost somewhere between $2,500 and $10,000. However, the exact costs depend on a range of variables. It’s best to discuss your specific needs with a professional web design company to get an accurate quote for your project.

If you’re on a tight budget you can consider using a website builder or template but we don’t recommend it.

### What is the best content management system for a roofing website?

A Content Management System (CMS) is software that allows you to create, manage, and publish content on your website without requiring extensive knowledge of coding.

There are many content management systems available in 2023 but **we recommend using WordPress**.

WordPress powers over 40% of all website on the internet and the best part is, it’s free to use.

If you are considering a different CMS make sure the software is updated regularly and user friendly. The last thing you want to worry about is struggling with adding new content to your roofing website.

### How many pages should a roofing website have?

To answer this question we first need to understand your goals with the roofing website.

Do you just need an informational website?

Are you looking to rank on Google and generate leads?

Depending on what you want to accomplish with your website will help to determine how many pages it should have.

If you’re looking for a simple website that is not focused on marketing and generating leads we recommend starting with a five page website. These pages would include; homepage, service, work gallery, about page and contact page.

If you’re looking for your roofing website to crush the competition online and be a lead tool for your business you are going to want 15-30 pages. This would include pages like a blog, reviews, city / keyword pages for seo, individual service pages etc.

### What's most important when hiring a company to design my roofing website?

It’s important for you to do your research and hire a company that can handle your roofing web design project professionally.

We think the most important thing to consider when hiring is does the company have experience designing websites for roofers or construction related websites.

Once you’ve vetted a company’s expertise it really boils down to cost and hiring a company you feel confident in their services.

### Do I need to know how to code to manage my roofing website?

If you have your roofing website built on WordPress you will not need to know how to code to manage it. You will need some general knowledge of WordPress and how the CMS functions. Your website designer should be able to provide you with training or answer any questions you have. Because WordPress is such a popular CMS there is millions of videos and articles on how to use it.

You will need to know how to resize and crop images. but beyond that, managing it should be easy.

### Can I expect to rank on Google with a new roofing website design?

If you don’t already have search engine rankings than its unlikely a new website will result in getting you rankings.

Your website should show up for searches about your company name but ranking for competitive keywords will require you to hire an search engine optimization (SEO) expert.

With that being said, you still want to make sure the website you are investing in is built with seo best practices. This will help you down the road if you decide to invest in an seo strategy.
